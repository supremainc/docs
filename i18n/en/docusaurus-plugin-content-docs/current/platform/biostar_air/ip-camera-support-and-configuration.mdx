BioStar Air automatically detects IP cameras connected to the same network as Airfob Edge. Because the cameras use the device as a gateway, a network video recorder (NVR) is not required. Instead, the cameras use the built-in SD card storage.

With an Airfob Edge or XPass 2 installed, BioStar Air can display the following concurrent live view streams.

* Up to two cameras at Full HD resolution (1080p)

* Up to four cameras at HD resolution (720p)

These specifications double when XStation 2 is used.

:::info

If recorded video does not play after registering the camera in BioStar Air, refer to [the following](video-troubleshooting).

:::

## Compatibility {#compatibility}

* **Onvif profiles**: The IP camera must be compatible with Onvif profile S or G.

  * **Onvif profile S**: Live video streaming

  * **Onvif profile G**: Recorded video stored on the SD card

* **SD card**: The IP camera must have a built-in SD card so that recorded footage can be played back later.

  :::note

  Use SanDisk microSDXC Class10 Max Endurance 256 GB or 512 GB.

  :::

* **Encoding**: The camera must support H.264 (AVC) and it must be set as the active codec. BioStar Air currently does not support H.265.

* **Recommended brands**: HanwhaVision, Hikvision, and Dahua cameras are highly compatible. Compatibility with other brands may vary depending on Onvif profile compliance. For more information on the tested and verified devices list, refer to [the following](#tested-devices).

* Contact the service team to test other camera brands.

## Functionality overview {#functionality}

Perform the following actions in the BioStar Air portal or mobile app.

* View live camera feeds.

* View recorded camera footage.

* Access camera footage related to access events.

## Instructions {#instructions}

<Steps>
<Step>

### Connect IP cameras

* **Network connection**: Ensure that the IP camera is on the same network as the Airfob Edge or Airfob Edge Ultimate.

* **Enable Onvif**: Make sure Onvif is enabled on the camera.

* **Device registration**: Make sure the device is registered in the BioStar Air portal.

</Step>
<Step>

### Log in to BioStar Air

**Master** administrators can change site settings such as enabling Video Management. Log in to the [BioStar Air portal](https://admin.biostarair.com/) with **master** privileges.

</Step>
<Step>

### Enable video management

1. Click <Cmd sid="settings_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="site_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar.

2. Click the <Cmd sid="site_setting_qr_reader_tab" product="air" /> tab.

3. Enable <Cmd sid="site_setting_video_management" product="air" />.

<Image src="/img/air/bsair-settings-site-addtional-feature-video-mangement.png" />

:::info

Only **master**-level administrators can enable this feature.

:::

</Step>
<Step>

### Add camera

1. Click <Cmd sid="device_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="cameras_groups_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar.

   <Image src="/img/air/bsair-device-camera.png" />

2. Click <Cmd sid="cameras_list_title_add_button" product="air" /> at the top right of the screen.

3. When the <Cmd sid="cameras_templ_title_modal" product="air" /> window appears, select the device that will act as the gateway.

   <Image src="/img/air/bsair-device-camera-add.png" caption />

4. Set the following information to add the camera.

   * <Cmd sid="cameras_add_camera_input_name" product="air" />: Enter the camera name.

   * <Cmd sid="recent_action_device_column" product="air" />: Select a name to identify the camera.

   * <Cmd sid="cameras_add_camera_input_id" product="air" />: Enter the ID to connect to the camera.

   * <Cmd sid="cameras_add_camera_input_pass" product="air" />: Enter the password to connect to the camera.

5. Click <Cmd sid="save_btn" product="air" /> to add the selected camera.

:::info

* The device must run firmware version 2.5.1 or later and be on the same network as the camera.

* BioStar Air automatically discovers cameras on the network.

:::

</Step>
<Step>

### Link to a door

Link the added camera to a door to associate video with access events.

1. Click <Cmd sid="door_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="door_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar.

2. Click the door in the list to assign a camera.

3. When the <Cmd sid="edit_door.title" product="air" /> panel appears, scroll to <Cmd sid="door_auxiliary_device" product="air" />.

4. Select the camera you added from <Cmd sid="door_product_column6" product="air" />.

   <Image src="/img/air/bsair-door-assign-camera.png" />

5. Click <Cmd sid="save_btn" product="air" /> to save the changes.

</Step>
<Step>

### View live camera video

View live video from the added cameras. Click <Cmd sid="monitoring_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="realtime_video_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar.

<Image src="/img/air/bsair-monitoring-realtime-camera.png" caption />

* View up to two Full HD (1080p) live streams per installed Airfob Edge.

* Add more devices or reduce camera resolution to view more live streams.

   > <Cmd sid="device_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="cameras_groups_submenu" product="air" /> → Click camera in the list → <Cmd sid="cameras_edit_camera_name_profile" product="air" />

</Step>
<Step>

### View recorded camera video

View recorded video streams. Click <Cmd sid="monitoring_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="recorded_video_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar.

<Image src="/img/air/bsair-monitoring-recorded-camera.png" caption />

</Step>
<Step>

### View event video

Click <Cmd sid="monitoring_menu" product="air" /> → <Cmd sid="event_logs_submenu" product="air" /> in the left sidebar. Find an event log with an associated video clip and click play to watch it.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Enable ONVIF on an IP camera

1. Open a web browser and enter the camera's IP address.

2. Authenticate with administrator credentials and log in.

3. Go to the **Network** or **Configuration** section.

4. Enable ONVIF or the open network video interface.

5. Create an ONVIF-only user profile if necessary.

6. Save the settings and test the ONVIF connection in BioStar Air.

:::tip

* **Firmware update**: Update the camera and the device to the latest firmware.

* **Port forwarding**: Configure port forwarding for ONVIF if required.

* **Third-party tools**: Use ONVIF Device Manager to verify camera settings and functionality.

:::
